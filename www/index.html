#1238

Today, we would remind you of our collective efforts to rebuild, reemploy and reconstitute ourselves as a nation. While our efforts to establish a solid infrastructure within the city have taken many steps forward in the last few years, we have not yet reached our goals. The storms continue to challenge our construction and renewal efforts. We all must re-dedicate ourselves daily, but with a critical eye towards our actions’ value. Consider the broad implications of how you choose to contribute to our collective goals. Enabling the antiquated behaviors of pre-storm society benefit none but the weak. In the upcoming days, we will be releasing updates on the Council’s progress on several important initiatives, including the Survey of Downed Power Distribution Networks and updates to the credits afforded in the Domiciliary Act. We look forward to updating you on our growth. I remain the city’s humblest servant.

#1241

A Reminder on Transmitter Maintenance.

In order to provide the highest level of service and security to the remaining populous during our continued time of crisis, government issued transmitters must be worn and on at all times. Owing to the fragile nature of our infrastructure and the persistent unpredictability of the storms, we ask you to comply with the following important maintenance procedures at least once a week.

  • Clean sand from speakers, buttons and knobs with a toothbrush and light spray of water.
  • Send a text based message at your scheduled test time.
  • Avoid placing your device on any materials known to retain static electricity: Glass, Aluminum, Wool and Fur.
  • If multiple surges have affected your device, report to your local police station for evaluation and source replacement.

Stay Safe, Stay Connected. Watch for alerts and avoid storm sites.

#1244

A Message from Secretary Hampstead regarding updates on additional benefits attendant to the Domiciliary act.

As per decisions made by Mayor Felton Stokes and the council, please note the following additional benefits recently added to Act #49, commonly known as the Domiciliary Act, with the ratification of amendment #19 on the 615th day after evacuation. Marrying couples in which both members are under the age of 23 will now receive and additional $100 stipend upon passing evaluations of their six-month partnership. Rations for women who are pregnant or have recently given birth, who are living with a legally bound partner will be increased by an extra 15% weekly. Recently married couples who rent or purchase property above 30ft and demonstrate satisfactory maintenance of such property will become eligible for 24.6% rebate in property taxes if filed within the appropriate time period. For more on the benefits that you can claim with the Domiciliary Act, please see your local officers.
